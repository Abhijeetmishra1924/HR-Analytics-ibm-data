### Executive Summary
This report presents an in-depth exploratory data analysis (EDA) of the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset, containing records of 1,470 employees across 35 attributes. The overall attrition rate stands at **16.12%** (237 employees).  

Key findings reveal that attrition is significantly higher among employees who work overtime, travel frequently, have low job/environment/work-life satisfaction, are single, younger (under 30), in entry-level roles, or work in Sales and Human Resources departments. Roles such as Sales Representative (41% attrition) and Laboratory Technician (24%) are particularly affected.  

Targeted interventions in work-life balance, overtime reduction, satisfaction improvement, and retention programs for high-risk groups can substantially reduce turnover and associated costs.

### Key Findings

#### Overall Attrition Rate
**16.12%** (237 out of 1,470 employees).

#### Top Drivers of Attrition
- **OverTime**: 30% attrition (Yes) vs. 8% (No).
- **Business Travel**: 25% (Frequent) vs. 8% (Non-Travel).
- **Marital Status**: 25% (Single) vs. 12% (Married) vs. 10% (Divorced).
- **Age**: ~25% for employees under 30 vs. ~6% for over 50.
- **Job Role**: Highest in Sales Representative (41%), Laboratory Technician (24%), Human Resources (19%).
- **Department**: Sales (21%), Human Resources (19%), Research & Development (14%).
- **Monthly Income**: Leavers average $4,780 vs. $6,830 for stayers; 25% attrition below $5,000.
- Satisfaction metrics (Job, Environment, Work-Life, Relationship) consistently show 20–25% attrition at low levels vs. 9–13% at high levels.

### Conclusion
Attrition is driven by a mix of workload (overtime, travel), compensation, satisfaction levels, and demographic factors. High-risk profiles include young, single employees in sales/technical roles working overtime with low satisfaction. Addressing these factors offers the greatest opportunity to reduce turnover.

---

### Recommendations
1. **Limit overtime** and promote flexible scheduling.
2. **Improve work-life balance** through policies and monitoring.
3. **Enhance satisfaction** via regular surveys, feedback loops, and recognition programs.
4. **Targeted retention** for Sales Representatives, Laboratory Technicians, and entry-level staff (mentorship, career paths, competitive pay).
5. **Reduce travel burden** with remote options or allowances.
6. **Focus compensation reviews** on lower-income and high-risk groups.

Implementing these measures can meaningfully lower the 16.12% attrition rate and improve organizational stability.
